Chapter 9: The Descent

Ishaan couldn’t sleep. Couldn’t eat. Couldn’t breathe without feeling like the walls were listening.

The photo. The knife. The records. They proved everything — and yet, *everyone* looked through him.

His professors smiled with pity. His classmates whispered like he’d gone mad. And the shadows… the shadows moved when he wasn’t looking.

He stopped attending classes. Stopped answering calls. He began drawing the symbols. On his walls. On his skin.

“They’re watching,” he murmured. “They want me to forget too.”
But he wouldn’t. He *couldn’t*.
